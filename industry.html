Introduction

The information included in this section has been derived from a number of sources, as mentioned in the references. Initially the Burning Glass data supplied as part of the assignment (above) has been interpreted with regard to the team’s ideal jobs. Further to this, an exploration of the current state of the industry has been carried out, with particular attention being paid to the transformative changes in the industry in the years since the Burning Glass data was published.

The data paints a picture of a thriving industry which is growing in most areas, with particularly strong growth being apparent in the areas of software development. The shift to cloud computing has seen big changes in how networks are being configured, and the growth of data centres is likely continue to lead to a rapidly changing future for network engineers. The data centre services market is, in fact, projected to grow from $48.9 billion in 2020 to $105.6 billion in 2026. (Mordor Intelligence, 2022) As this transition towards cloud based infrastructure continues it is likely that network engineering positions will continue to see high levels of demand, with many opportunities arising within the tech giants that provide this infrastructure.(Kyler Middleton, 2019)

Each group member has reviewed the interpretation of the data and reflected on their choice of career path, with opinions and comments included at the conclusion of the summary.

Group Generic Skills

Chart 2 demonstrates the employer demand for our required generic skill set. This data shows that there is extremely strong demand for good communication skills, with an even spread of demand for the remaining skills. There is more overlap with a number of generic skills as opposed to IT skills, with Analytical Skills and Problem Solving ranked as being of importance for every position.

It is worth noting that the data may be somewhat skewed, as it is derived from job adverts. It is unlikely employers would list some of the qualities that are intrinsically part of the job, as it would be assumed that candidates would be aware of the importance of these skills. For example, a knowledge of Git is listed as something only 2% of employers are looking for, but it would be assumed that anybody applying for a developer position would be familiar with version control.

The three highest ranking IT skills not identified in our group’s required skill set are “Project “Management”, “SAP” and “Business Management”.

The three highest ranking generic skills not identified in our group’s required skill set are “Writing”, “Detail Oriented” and “Research”.


Job Opportunities & Employment Numbers

There is a high demand for Software Developers, Software Engineers and Network Engineers. This is due to mainly the world moving online, whether it be individual, an offline business, education domain to digital platforms. Especially after the global covid-19 pandemic, digital presence has been a high priority for business and individuals alike. So, tech companies need skilled software engineers/developers and network engineers to help create/build ideal software solutions, the ability to house large data/storage systems and the need for exceptional network engineers. Service Desk Analysts and Systems Administrators are a close 2nd & 3rd in job opportunities, as the need for everything to be ‘online’ companies and/or individuals are seeking these types of roles to respond to incoming requests for IT support and resolve network, hardware and software issues. Seeing as Software Developers (security specialisation) role is a more specialised field of work, there wasn’t much information to obtain, but ever-increasing demand for specific computer programs with an eye towards safeguarding computer systems and data/information will never decrease.


Projected Growth % 5 years

As you can see, there is a huge potential growth for software developers/engineers and network engineers. As our reliance on technology grows, from online shopping for clothes, to ording food for delivery. As such, there is a massive need for more online support, to assist with the development, implementation and maintenance of these support infrastructures. As more and more companies delve into the online market, companies are becoming more reliant on computer networks to share data, programs, files and any digital information this makes network engineers are more prominent role within an organisation using computerized system to do its work.
