Blockchain and Cryptocurrency

What does it do?

A blockchain is a distributed database where information is stored in ‘blocks’ that, once filled, get attached to previous blocks in such a way that it could be described as a chain. When a block is attached to the chain, it is secured via cryptography or encryption thus the information cannot be altered, deleted or destroyed however it can still be viewed and added to.

To participate in blockchain networks an individual would require a device capable of accessing a web browser and an internet connection, such as a mobile phone, a personal computer or tablet pc. For the aforementioned devices to be used as a node (colloquially referred to as a mining machine) it would require an internet connection and a substantial graphics card to process the encryption required.

Public blockchains allow anyone to set up their system as a network node to process additions to the blockchain and as such, the security needs to be much more intense than a private blockchain where the nodes are trusted. An example of a public Blockchain’s security would be cryptography or an encryption along with a consensus system between nodes. With this security system, the nodes must agree on what data is the correct data. This makes malicious attacks almost impossible because it would require an attack on the majority of the blockchain’s nodes to fool the consensus system.

The way to maliciously effect a blockchain need to be done by firstly corrupting the newest block and attacked up the chain. However, the consensus system requires a 51% majority vote from all the nodes within the network that the content within the timestamps and information is correct. This makes corrupting information within a blockchain a very arduous and lengthy task that consumers copious amounts of resources to complete. Also, in assistance to this, the expansion of blockchain networks has resulted in successfully hacking into the chain to be an insurmountable task as the number of nodes now ranges in the thousands.

The Consensus System security feature has given users the ability to lock data in such a way that it has resulted the storage of these ledges and transactions to be so valuable that has become a currency itself. This currency is referred to as a form of cryptocurrency.

Although any data point can be stored in the blocks of a blockchain, at time of authoring this report, the main use of this technology is for the creation of currency.

Although, an example where Blockchain Technology has been used as something other than currency, is where it is used in the food industry to keep an unalterable ledger that tracks food from its source to the consumer destination. This verifies claims of ‘Ethically Sourced,’ ‘Organic’ and/or ‘Fair Trade.’ The implementation is also used to trace contamination through each step during the manufacturing process until the contaminant can be located. Companies such as IBM, Unilever and Walmart have already implemented this technology into their supply chains with many more developing their own solution.

Also, some future theories as the potential application of blockchain technology has been to store votes for a federal election in such a way as to be pure and untampered. Under ideal conditions, theorized by experts, this application would incorporate a public network to remove the ability for a government to own all the nodes and grant them access to manipulate the stored data. It has been suggested that a more likely implementation would be a provided private network with the government owning the network nodes, which is far less secure but would require less participation from the voting populace to function.

What is the most likely impact?

There is a significant cost associated with people setting up public nodes. however, there are many people getting involved with “mining” bitcoin and it has resulted in an economic shortage of computer parts, particularly in graphics cards which are the main processing power behind building the cryptography in the blockchain. This is in part due to the hardware suppliers such as Nvidia not having enough logistical infrastructure in place to meet this new economic demand.

A consequence of this is that many people who require graphics cards to perform within their occupations have become very irate due to the shortage of hardware and the sudden increase in demand causing a scarcity which has raised the prices significantly.

Some parts manufacturers have tried to alleviate these shortages by creating “mining specific” graphics cards to allow for the main graphics cards to be in less demand. However, these parts have been halted due to a global chip shortage.

A Social Consequence of these shortages is that people who depend on main graphics to complete their occupations have become very irate and resistant to the idea of block chain technology as it has caused a logistical burden on their professions.

Another reason for Blockchain currencies not being adopted in professional environments is that it does not use a “Know your customer” (KYC) system and thus transactions are anonymous even though they are publicly visible. This has been exploited for illicit purposes and will continue to be used in this way as blockchain and cryptocurrency do not require a third party to utilise. An example would be deep-web websites such as silk road where a user can purchase drugs with cryptocurrency.

Machine Learning

What Does it do?

“Machine learning is a branch of artificial intelligence (AI) and computer science which focuses on the use of data and algorithms to imitate the way that humans learn, gradually improving its accuracy” (IBM Cloud Education, 2020).

This is accomplished in numerous ways involving the way data is input into the AI. For Example, ‘Supervised Learning’ is where an information is input into the machine and the machine ‘guesses’ what that input represents. Then, a supervisor can mark the result produced as a success, or a failure. With this feedback the machine can learn how to guess correctly. There are many sub-divisions of this method, such as reward-based learning, where correct responses are rewarded a higher score and the machine is told to get the highest score possible. The other approach is unsupervised learning, where a computer is left to assume the level of relevance between data and infer correlation on its own. Currently, unsupervised learning models are used for clustering and feature reduction.

Machine learning, in its current stage, is refining and advancing the capabilities and applications of AI technologies by making them learn the same way that humans do. The result of this is that the machine will be able to learn from its environment or provided data, then furtherly classify the information, then finally learn, and develop its actions from its successes and failures.

Machine learning is currently used in industry to sort through or extract useful data from a large data set. This is because machines have been proven to be much more capable of working through persistent workloads.

This results in a significant boost in business productivity being achieved through use of this technology. Machine learning has also been proven to be able to work through large data sets such as market information for large companies that service customers globally with greater efficiency and accuracy.

One particular use for machine learning is market prediction. Market prediction systems can be trained to assess relevant market data and furtherly predict, within a certain margin of error, what the market is going to do in the future, by recognising patterns in market fluctuations. This simplification of assessing the market, which is large and complex to become much more obtainable to be assessed and as a result more significant and accurate data is supplied to businesses and investors to make more informed decisions.

Contrary to this, certain ethical implications have been expressed by people and has resulted in them to be reluctant to rely on an AI for economical or monetary advice. However, there has been notable success by independent parties adopting and applying these prediction algorithms to cryptocurrency.

Another use for Machine learning, is that it has also been used for the simulation of materials. This meaning with the use of machine learning in such a way that it is possible to train an AI to simulate hair follicles or falling sand in a way that mirrors reality effectively. This in future will be especially useful for Hollywood effects if a practical effect is too expensive or difficult to achieve without simulation.

The YouTube channel, "Two Minute Papers", is a channel that studies machine learning, provides further insight on these simulations.

The Software company ‘OpenAI’ is leading experimental applications of machine learning. It has been used for experiments such as, simulating a game of ‘Hide and Seek’ between AI controlled participants or asking a computer to draft a poem which has resulted in researchers getting unpredictable but coherent responses. Studies have proven that together, machine learning and AI can be a powerful framework to develop the tools of the future. Tools which will have the potential to produce new and ground-breaking ways to carry out tasks that exceeds the limits of human capability.

Neural networks are machine learning with applied neuroscience and work by creating neurons that do simple calculations and then networking them into other neurons for further processing of information, this works remarkably like a human brain and one notable result of this approach is adaptability, in that a neural network can be handed multiple sets of data and crunch through them by adapting new rules or neurons for each data set. This approach to machine learning requires a fair bit of training but once trained are far more powerful than older forms of trained algorithm

Machine learning has the power to do many things, it is expected that the animation industry will benefit from this technology as it will make rendering complex 3D (3 Dimensional) animations much more resource effective, saving on time rendering, increasing the accuracy of the render, and saving electricity in doing so.

Industry data sets will become incredibly manageable with an AI over-seeing the categorization and overall organisation of massive datasets, which will allow for much greater corporate control over their products and services.

Advertising has also become much better since the advent of targeted advertising algorithms most of which are fuelled by learning AI. There are privacy concerns risen from the data aggregation required to train the advert delivery systems however it is an AI looking through the data and not humans, thus ethical concerns are dismissed as current legislation in those areas is unsure how to react to these emergent technologies.

Most trades done on the stock market are done algorithmically to minimise losses and maximise profits, however the human element in trading is not lost as hybrid systems where algorithms and human discretion are used simultaneously exist and continue to be the most sustainable option for the future of the market. This may make higher education in these fields focus more on data science applications where humans and AI can coexist.

Machine learning also appears to be focused on making any task for a computer more efficient, it may be predicted that super-computers may only need the raw power in our mobile phones to run complex equations and simulations, because the technology just gets increasingly efficient as time passes

Machine learning algorithms drive a lot of social media today, thus every ad that is delivered to your screen and every post you see is curated by media engines that are designed to keep you on their website. Clouds, Services, Servers

The IT technology that has been selected is ‘Clouds, services, servers’, which can referred to under the umbrella term ‘cloud computing’. ‘The cloud’ is a remnant of IT industry slang from when technical diagrams of servers and network infrastructure for the Internet were drawn as a cloud. Moving ‘to the cloud’ became a way to refer to computing processes that were taking place on the servers-and-infrastructure part of the Internet. (Cloudflare, 2022)

What does it do?

What is the state of the art of this new technology?

The state of the art of cloud computing as stated by (Zhang, 2010) is described in terms of the key technologies and the current popular cloud computing products.

  • Architectural design of data centres: the design for a cloud computing data centre should endeavour to meet the following goals
    • Uniform high capacity – The “maximum rate of a server-to-server traffic flow should be limited only by the available capacity on the network-interface cards of the sending and receiving servers” (Zhang, 2010)
    • Free VM (Virtual Machine) migration – “Virtualization allows the entire VM state to be transmitted across the network to migrate a VM from one physical machine to another. The communication topology should be designed so as to support rapid virtual machine migration.” (Zhang, 2010)
    • Resiliency – “The network infrastructure must be fault-tolerant against various types of server failures, link outages, or server-rack failures.” (Zhang, 2010)
    • Scalability – “The network infrastructure must be able to scale to a large number of servers and allow for incremental expansion.” (Zhang, 2010)
    • Backward compatibility – “The network infrastructure should be backward compatible with switches and routers running Ethernet and IP.” (Zhang, 2010)
  • Modular data centre (MDC) is another area of fast innovation within the industry, using thousands of servers to form the network infrastructure, being interlinked by switches. These aid in rapid response times as well as helping reduce redundancy. (Zhang, 2010)
  • Distributed file system over clouds such as Google File System (GFS) divide files into chunks of 64 megabytes to provide effective and dependable access to data, adjusted for running on data centres giving extremely high data throughputs with low latency and failures. (Zhang, 2010)
  • Distributed application framework over clouds – “HTTP-based applications usually conform to some web application framework such as Java EE. In modern data centre environments, clusters of servers are also used for computation and data-intensive jobs such as financial trend analysis, or film animation.” (Zhang, 2010)
Popular and dominant cloud computing products are:
  • Amazon Web Services (AWS)
  • Microsoft Windows Azure platform
  • Google App Engine
As this article is from 2010, some of the details may be out of date, but can assume that the points made still stand as it is referring to the key technologies that make up cloud computing, there just may be some more recent ones missing.

What can be done now?

There are many things that cloud computing can do at the moment. Some of these (but by no means all of them) are:

  • Cloud Storage – Services like Dropbox, Google Drive and OneDrive allow for the storage, access, and retrieval of files via an Internet enabled user interface. (IBM Cloud Team, IBM Cloud, 2020)
  • Data Backup – The cloud offers a convenient alternative to using physical mediums such as hard drives to back up data for retrieval should the original data be lost. (IBM Cloud Team, IBM Cloud, 2020)
  • Big Data Analytics - By utilising cloud computing there is the ability to use big data analytics to leverage massive amounts of both structured and unstructured data to harness the benefit of obtaining business value such as evaluating customer buying habits to create targeted advertising and marketing. (IBM Cloud Team, IBM Cloud, 2020)
  • Infrastructure as a service (IaaS) - IaaS comprises of the main building blocks for cloud IT. Generally, it offers access to networking features, computers (virtual or on dedicated hardware), operating systems and data storage space on a pay-as-you-go-basis. (Amazon Web Services, 2022) (Microsoft Azure, 2022)
  • • Platform as a service (PaaS) - PaaS refers to cloud computing services that provide an on-demand setting for developing, testing, delivering, and managing software applications. PaaS is designed to eliminate the need for the organisation of underlying infrastructure (usually hardware and operating systems), and lets developers focus on the deployment and running of web or mobile apps, without worrying about setting up the servers, storage, network, and databases needed. (Amazon Web Services, 2022) (Microsoft Azure, 2022)
  • Software as a service (SaaS) - SaaS is a method for providing software applications over the Internet, on request and typically on a subscription basis. With SaaS, cloud providers host and manage the software application and core infrastructure, and handle any upkeep, like software upgrades and security patching. Users can log into and use a SaaS application from any compatible device over the Internet. The actual application runs in cloud servers that may be far removed from a user's location. A SaaS application may be accessed through a browser or through an app. Online email applications such as Gmail and Office 365, are common examples of SaaS applications. (Amazon Web Services, 2022) (Cloudflare, 2022) (Microsoft Azure, 2022)
Other as-a-service cloud offerings include (but aren’t limited to):
  • Function as a Service (FaaS)/Serverless Computing
  • Artificial Intelligence as a Service (AIaaS)
  • Network as a Service (NaaS)
  • Workspace as a Service (WaaS)
  • Database as a Service (DBaaS)
  • Firewall as a Service (FWaaS)
  • Identity as a Service (IDaaS)

What is likely to be able to do be done soon (say in the next 3 years)?

Some of the things that cloud computing could likely be able to do or facilitate in the near future are:

  • Quantum computing – Quantum computers use the tenets of quantum physics to execute intricate algorithm calculations and handle huge datasets in rapid time. In quantum computing, data is stored in qubits, making the process much faster, as qubits are a simpler form of data.
  • Automation – The cloud can be used to handle tedious and time-consuming tasks which both frees up staff from performing the tasks manually and lowers the cost of operations by requiring less staff overall. (Saleem, 2020)
  • Internet of Things (IoT) – IoT devices can utilise cloud computing as it offers high speed, performance, flexibility, and sufficient storage space to keep the data safe, find resources, and share information among different users within the same space. (Saleem, 2020)
  • Modular Software Development – To be able to utilize the cloud technology, code and applications have to break down into smaller components which are not connected with one another. This makes it simpler for developers to upload their works into the cloud and makes accessing it from remote locations easier too.
  • Improved performance – As the use of cloud computing and the amount of ways in which it is used increase, the speed of the networks and how efficiently data is analysed, received and delivered will also increase to meet user expectations and demands. (DataFlair , 2022)

What technological or other developments make this possible?

Cloud computing is made feasible through virtualization. Management software called a hypervisor is installed on physical servers to link up and virtualize them: abstracting their collective resources and merging them together to generate virtual servers. These virtual resources can then be automated and distributed over the cloud for collective use in a single organization or across numerous organizations. (VMware, 2022 )

Robotics

What Does it Do?

Robotics, defined as “robotics, design, construction, and use of machines (robots) to perform tasks done traditionally by human beings.” (Encyclopaedia Britannica, 2021). Robots are used in a wide range of uses such as automobile manufacturing, entertainment, healthcare, and transport. Many Robots possess AI (Artificial Intelligence) to complete their task as it allows them to have self-learning and controlling capabilities. With diversification, robots do not just take a humanoid shape as they are shaped in many ways to complete their varied tasks and those shaped in a humanoid figure are determined as androids.

This definition both displays a short display of the uses of robotics but also exemplifies the diversification of robotics since it earliest known examples seen around 1495 in Leonardo da Vinci’s note books which displayed a basic model of a knight in armour that was able to stand up and move basic parts of its body. (See Diagram 1.0).

Since this time, many discoveries have been made around robotics that has advanced in areas such as AI (Artificial Intelligence), Engineering and Software Development have forced people to redefine more complexly what a robot is. This has furtherly resulted in the term “android” to be used to define humanoid looking robots.

As of 2022 we have seen several robots be used commercially to improve the rate of production in many industries such as manufacturing. For Example, since their introduction in the Automotive Industry to produce cars, this has resulted in companies such as Ford to be able to assemble cars at 15% faster rate than previously (Will Knight, 2021) (See Diagram 2.0)

Robotics have also been introduced into regular households in the form of smalls robots that assist humans in a several tasks such as mowing lawns, providing entertainment, cleaning out house as well as also providing emotional analysis and support. A popular example of a robot that has been widely adopting by households is the “Roomba.” This robot has been developed to successfully scan a household and regularly vacuum and clean the floors of residences by following a blueprint of the house that has been generated by the robot to avoid collisions. The effectiveness of Roombas in comparison to manual cleaning is still debated but has been suggested by several articles to be superior to a regular household vacuum cleaner.
