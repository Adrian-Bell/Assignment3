Group Relflection

After reflecting on how we went with our Assessment 2 work, we understood what our strengths and weaknesses were regarding our work and were able to utilise what works best for us and what we needed to work on. Group communication was one of the vital lessons learned during the first group project. Overall, the group gelled together well, and we had no issues or conflicts. During meetings we kept things light and moving but could sometimes get off track with tangential conversations.

We combatted this by creating a to-do list and holding each other more accountable for tasks. This aspect of our collaborative work was an idea by our unofficial lead Kieran to host a live/editable word document. All work would be inputted and reviewed for the whole group’s workload. This way of working worked well with us tremendously since we were able to share our work and progressively see the group’s contribution and assist in any alterations or advice on what to incorporate into the report. We were able to divide and conquer our individual assigned tasks and assign them to those who best fit the task at hand. This allowed us to complete a large amount of our prototyping quickly and thus sped the development process of the project along quite nicely.

For the groups’ weaknesses, the relatively smaller scope of the project may have been such as too limiting in the number of artifacts we could produce overall. Therefore, not everyone could be completing multiple unique tasks for the report. This was a choice made early on, deciding to create a functioning prototype instead of a host of comparatively inconsequential artifacts. In hindsight, multiple planned features could have been discussed and prototyped for future implementation to have a more varied workload.

I think all members of K.A.A.L.A.B have grown over the past couple of months and have begun to assess their own strengths and weaknesses when considering group efforts at the beginning of burgeoning IT careers. The overall foray has been a positive one in group work, and we will carry this experience forwards into future group activities.
