Group Reflection

Summary

As a group, K.A.A.L.A.B. has generated a project that we are all rightly proud of. As expected there have been some teething problems as we all settled into the routine of not only collaborative work but university work in general. We have overcome the issues raised by multiple time zones, work schedules, family commitments etc. to form a productive collaborative environment. Moving forward, we have the potential to continue to work well together to produce more high-quality work as we look to further the Mythos project.

Our overall technical skills have improved greatly over the past month, and we have developed a workflow where each person’s strengths and weaknesses are complemented by other members of the team. This will give us a better understanding of which tasks to assign to each person or persons to achieve the desired result.

Our records also need to be addressed. We failed to maintain any meeting minutes or actions moving from meeting to meeting so there was no accountability for each member. We constantly had to remind each other of what task we were working on, and this might have dragged the more cooperative tasks to a standstill.

Looking over at our individual reflections on how we performed as a group, there are a few common themes that occur, and they are time management, organisation, and structure.

Team organisation and structure can be addressed by having a ‘Group Leader’. This will help solidify who is working on which task and what expectations are set for the group as a whole. Everyone will know what is expected of them by the group leader, which leads to fewer misunderstandings about what to do. Moving forward, we would need to implement team goals and objectives, some strict ground rules, task allocation and task maintenance.

On reflection, there are definitely areas of concern moving forward when we start our Assessment 3 project, some fine-tuning in one area and major fixings in another. So ideally, we would like to be open and honest regarding these issues, so as to remove future misunderstandings or frustrations with group work. Communication is the utmost key ingredient now (with accountability coming a close second) to determine whether we fail or succeed. In conclusion, what matters most is what we have learnt from the first phase of group work, what worked well, what didn’t work and how we addressed it so as to learn from our mistakes and grow from strength to strength.
